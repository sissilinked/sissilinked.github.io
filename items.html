The Items

We chose 10 items that are somehow connected to events, concepts, people and places related to the figure of Elisabeth of Austria, by mixing archival documents, bibliographic records and artefacts. All ten items come from cultural institutions and aggregators that were considered reliable sources for our project. These institutions are also the providers of the items’ description, which is briefly introduced here, and they include information about people, places, dates and subjects. This section of the site will allow you to acquire a deeper understanding of the material culture related to Sissi’s figure and to start questioning how they can be connected.

Photograph

"Elisabeth, Empress of Austria"
(1867)

A carte-de-visite photographic portrait of Elisabeth of Austria in the day of her coronation as Queen of Hungary.
From the National Portrait Gallery.

Lithograph

"Ferenc József I and his family in Gödöllő"
(1871)

Lithography depicting the Hungarian royal family at Gödöllő Palace. King Franz Joseph and Queen Elisabeth, with their children Rudolf, Marie Valerie and Gisela.
From the Hungarian National Museum.

Book

"Luccheni e l'antropologia criminale"
(1899)

An anthropological study on Elisabeth's murderer conducted by the famous Italian criminologist Cesare Lombroso.
From Sbn/Ubo.

  • Source

Dress

"Empress Elisabeth's black court dress"
(1885)

A black silk dress with lace trim and rich embroidery, that documents the tall, slender figure of the empress Elisabeth and her incredibly narrow, oval "Viennese wasp waist".
From the Kunsthistorisches Museum.

Newspaper

"Le Petit Parisien:journal quotidien du soir"
(1898)

The number of the prominent French newspaper Le Petit Parisien reporting the news of the assassination of the empress on its front page.
From Gallica, BnF.

  • Source

Carriage

"Personal Landaulet of Empress Elisabeth"
(1885)

A small black carriage,well suited for city traffic, specifically built for Empress Elisabeth, who often took it with her on trips and last used it before her murder on Lake Geneva.
From Europeana.

  • Source

Movie

"Sissi la giovane imperatrice"
(1956)

The second film of the successful and critically acclaimed Sissi Trilogy, directed by Ernst Marischka and starring Romy Schneider, who owns her breakthrough to the role played in this movie and in other where she continued to portrait Sissi.
From ICBSA.

  • Source

Book

"The Imperial Style: Fashions of the Hapsburg Era"
(1980)

A book based on the hugely successful exhibition Fashions of the Hapsburg Era: Austria-Hungary, held at The Metropolitan Museum of Art from December 1979 through August 1980.
From THE MET Library.

  • Source

Music

"Polonaise no. 3, op. 40, no. 1, A major"
(1838)

The famous military polonaise composed by Fryderyk Chopin, frequently played in Vienna during the Imperial times and featured in the soundtrack of the Sissi Trilogy.
From the British Library.
